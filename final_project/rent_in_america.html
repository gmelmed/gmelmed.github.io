Mapping America's Housing Crisis

Rent Inflation

The surge in rent prices is not simply the result of post-pandemic inflation. The chart below shows the year-on-year change in rent prices and inflation since 2016. Rent inflation outpaced overall inflation for years before the pandemic began. During the immediate recovery from the pandemic (late 2021-mid 2022), rent prices rose an order of magnidute faster than overall prices. Thankfully, rent inflation has slowed down, but it's still outpacing inflation in most places.

To build this chart, I scraped data from the Zillow's Observed Rent Index (ZORI). ZORI is my preferred rent statistic because ___. I used the FRED API to get the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for all urban consumers. I built the chart using vega-lite.

Mapping Rent

The rent crisis is not felt equally across the US. The map below shows the year-on-year change in rent prices in US counties, which are sized by population.Rent inflation is felt most accutely in the midwest and northeast. On the west coast, rent inflation is low. A small handful of counties, mostly in the Texas, saw their rent index decrease over the past year.

This chart uses the same Zillow data as the previous chart. I built the map using D3 and mapbox. The D3 spec for this map uses the most recent ZORI data for each county.
